MarshMallow Whale

                
 How many marshmallows can fit inside of a whale? ~ Zara
    
    Whales are big and marshmallows are very small. 
     
 For this question, let's pretend we're using a blue whale. The volume of an average blue whale depends on many factors but if we assumed that it was about 90 feet long and the maximum girth of 80 feet, we should get around 5,170,800 cubic centimeters.  


    For the marshmallow, we can pretend that it is a perfect cylinder with height 1 inch and diameter 1 inch to get (using pi * r^2 * height)  12.87 cubic centimeters. Now, with some simple division, we get around 401,771.5617715618 ≈ 401,712 marshmallows. Since there are about 4 grams of sugar per 7 grams of marshmallows, that is about 2,811,984 grams of sugar. Also, there are about 16 calories per 4 grams of sugar, there is 11,247,936 calories.  
                    
                                   
    Since humans need around 2,000 calories a day, you could have a 5,624 day meal. You just have to get an empty blue whale and a lot of marshmallows.

Ocean + human = Disaster

    1T: 0.000000000000059978 x 1T is pretty big. That’s 125 times bigger than 2024's current world population. But is that enough?? 0.059978 isn't making the cut. 

    1 quintillion: That is like really big! 59978 is a real step up from 1T but still, not the 321 million we were looking for. 

    Answer?: After some quick calculation, the answer is 5,351,962,386,208,276,368,001! This number goes up to the sextillions!! So in conclusion, you need as much people as there are grains of sand in the world!! Good luck recruiting that much people...
