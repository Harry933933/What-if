Termite Takeover

                    
 How termites do you need to destroy your home in 1 second? ~ Mom

For this question, I will use dimensions of an average house which are about 50 x 50 square feet, about 13 feet high, and about 0.3 feet thick. 
                                            
                                                    
 50 * 50 * 13 = 32,500 
                                                    
 The empty space inside of our house/cube is (50-0.3) (50-0.3) (13 – 0.3) = 31,370.143 
                                                
 32,500 – 31,370.143 = 1,129.857 
                                            
                                            
                                            
 Now that we got the volume....TERMITES!    
 
                     
                               
                    

About 60,000 termites can eat 5 grams of wood a day. Saying the termite type is the Eastern Subterranean termite, we can convert the 5 grams of wood to cubic feet which is about 0.0050853119864000210365(let's call this "Annoying Number or AN) cubic feet.  
                    
                     
                    
 1,129.857 / AN which is 222,180.468577  rounded. Multiplying that by our 60,000 termites give us 13,330,828,115 rounded. This gives us how much termites we need in a day. However, we need a second so we multiply that by how many seconds are in a day to get our answer, 1,151,783,549,136,000. 
This number gives us that if all the termites stand in a line, it will be 18,220,232,440 miles!!
  
 This is equivalent to going around Earth 731,707 times!!(Also the same as going to the Sun and to Pluto about 4.5 times.)

Biggest Shadow

                    
 What is the biggest shadow you can make from yourself? ~ Hyuby
    
    
    Two things: Time, and place. Let's start with something easy. The time. Obviously, at noon, you create the shortest shadow of yourself. This is mainly because the Sun is directly above you. So the more the Sun is leaning, the more longer and bigger of a shadow will appear which happens usually at sunrise, or sunset. So stand at a vast open field during sunset and you will create a long shadow. Shadows depend on the Sun ray to hit different angles. The question just asked for the biggest shadow and no specific place so how about we leave Earth? 

 
    Couple things to consider: The length of a shadow is calculated with the formula; x = h/tan(a). X is the shadow length, h is the height of the object, and a is the angle from the shadow to the Sun. After some more research, I found out that the "angle from the shadow to the Sun" is x = arctan(h/t) where h is the height of the object, x is the "angle from the shadow to the Sun", and t is the distance from the object to the Sun. 
                        
                                          
    Moon: Using the formula that was given to use (see 1.), we find that the shadow length is about 9.8669484658303137081 meters. I wrote "about" because since there are craters on the Moon, it can create bigger and/or smaller shadows. Also, the lack of an atmosphere creates disturbances on light movement. Either way, 9.8669484658303137081 meters is about the length of a U-Haul truck or a really tall guy(I will do this for all the planets...) 
     

                        
    Mercury: Since Mercury is closer to the Sun, it will create a smaller shadow. Using the formula, the shadow length is 0.9166695079989725 meters. This is nearly a 10th of the shadow in the Moon!! (Also about a human arm span or a VERY short person(I told you so...))
